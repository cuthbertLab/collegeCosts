The Costs

Click any name to learn more from the Dept. of Education. Costs include room and board but do not include the costs paid by people who did not apply for financial aid.

Colleges are sorted by SAT 25th percentile (not mean/median). Why? Too many of us see an average number and think, "well, I can't go there, my score is not high enough." Guess what? 50% of all students are below the median, so someone's got to get in. In fact, 1/4 of all students will have a below 25th percentile score, so it may certainly be worth applying even if your scores are below the numbers given there.

{dataGoesHere}

Note that some schools will show unusual "discontinuities" especially at levels where there aren't enough applicants to generate true averages (generally at the lowest and highest income levels). In some cases, people with the lowest incomes are listed as needing to pay more than the next higher level; my own interpretation of this data is that probably some well-off families are able to report little or no income (due to foreign investments, family trusts, etc.) and thus would be grouped with the lowest income families. If it seems too odd to be true, it probably is. (However, some of those amazing deals are true).

The lack of a feature to show all schools regardless of costs is on purpose. If a school is pricing itself way out of the range of average families, maybe it deserves to be ignored?
